# Governance

Cairn is a maintainer-led open-source project. Proposals and usage obstacles are discussed in
public issues and pull requests. Maintainers seek evidence and rough consensus, but the release
maintainer makes the final call when consensus is not possible.

Maintainers are responsible for triage, review, security response, dependency and release policy,
and protecting Cairn's local-first/privacy boundaries. Significant compatibility, security,
storage, release, or product decisions require an ADR or equivalent durable design record.

Conflicts should first be resolved in the relevant issue or pull request. A participant may ask a
maintainer not involved in the disagreement to mediate. Conduct matters are handled under
`CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md`; vulnerabilities follow `SECURITY.md` and must not be debated publicly before
coordinated disclosure.

Only maintainers may approve releases. A release requires reviewed source changes, passing required
checks, an annotated semantic-version tag, and approval of the protected publishing environment.
No individual may bypass the build-once artifact flow.

Maintainers may be added after sustained, constructive contributions and explicit review of the
least privileges needed. Removal may occur for inactivity, request, repeated policy violations, or
security risk. Repository access—not this document—is authoritative for current technical access.
